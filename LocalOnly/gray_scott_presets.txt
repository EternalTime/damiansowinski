Gray-Scott Reaction-Diffusion: Preset Parameter Notes
======================================================

Canonical normalization used on this site:
  Du = 0.2, Dv = 0.1 (ratio = 2), dt = 1
  Source: Pearson 1993 / Karl Sims parameterization

All (f, k) values below are for this normalization only.
Different sources use different Du/Dv (e.g. 1.0/0.5, 2e-5/1e-5),
and their (f,k) values are NOT interchangeable with ours.

Presets
-------
Solitons:  f=0.055, k=0.062  — stable self-replicating pulses, robust, easy to seed
Worms:     f=0.078, k=0.061  — stripe/worm morphology; well separated from soliton regime
Spots:     f=0.030, k=0.062  — stable spots; lower feed rate, slow to develop from seed
Mitosis:   f=0.037, k=0.065  — dividing spots (cell-division-like); f=0.0367, k=0.0649 also cited

Notes
-----
- Spots and Mitosis are slow to bootstrap from a small perturbation.
  The seed needs to survive long enough for the autocatalytic reaction to amplify v.
  A 5x5 pixel seed is marginal; larger seeds are more reliable.

- Worms at f=0.058, k=0.065 (previously used) is too close to the soliton
  regime and does not produce distinct worm morphology.

- Mitosis k value: 0.065 used here; 0.0649 also cited in literature.
  Difference is small but can matter near bifurcation boundaries.

- The (f,k) parameter space has a crescent-shaped active region between
  the Hopf curve and the static bifurcation curve (Pearson 1993).
  Outside this region the system goes to a homogeneous steady state.
